# bb-studio-ops

Operations is not administration. It's how the studio maintains quality, margin, and sanity.

---

## Core Principle

Bad ops kills good work. Unclear scope, missing contracts, and underpriced projects drain creative energy and damage client relationships. Set it up right at the start.

---

## Critical Reflex: Project Health Check

At any point in a project, ask:

| Question | Red flag if... |
|---|---|
| Is scope documented and agreed? | It was verbal only |
| Is timeline realistic? | It was set by the client, not the studio |
| Is pricing based on value or cost? | It was based on hours |
| Does the client know what's in and out of scope? | They've been asking for things "quickly" |
| Is there a clear decision-maker? | Multiple people giving conflicting feedback |

Any red flag = stop and recalibrate before continuing.

## Proposal Structure

```
1. SITUATION    — Show you understand their problem (not generic)
2. APPROACH     — How Brand & Bond specifically solves this
3. SCOPE        — Exactly what's included (and what's not)
4. DELIVERABLES — Specific outputs, not phases
5. TIMELINE     — Realistic, with client dependencies noted
6. INVESTMENT   — Pricing with options if applicable
7. NEXT STEP    — How to proceed
```

### Scope Definition Standard
Every scope item must answer:
- What is produced?
- How many rounds of revision are included?
- What does client need to provide for this to happen?
- What is NOT included?

Ambiguous scope is a guarantee of scope creep.

---

## Pricing Principles

**Price on value, not hours**
What is the outcome worth to the client? Price relative to that, not the hours it takes.

**Revision rounds are scope**
"Unlimited revisions" is not a service offer — it's a cost with no ceiling. Define rounds upfront.

**Options architecture**
When presenting pricing, 3 options outperforms 1:
- Entry scope (minimum viable)
- Recommended scope (optimal)
- Full scope (comprehensive)

Clients who see only one number negotiate it down. Clients who see options choose where to play.

---

## Push Back Triggers

**"Can you do it cheaper?"**
> I can do a smaller scope for a smaller investment. Here's what that looks like. What I can't do is the same scope for less — that means cutting corners and I won't do that.

**"We need it in half the time"**
> Half the time means half the scope or double the resource cost. Which direction do you want to go?

**"We'll sort the contract later"**
> The contract protects both of us. Let's do it now — it takes an hour and it prevents months of misunderstanding.
